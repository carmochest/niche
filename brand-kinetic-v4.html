01 — Die-cut system

Print gets a real hole.

Covers, posters, and ID sleeves are physically die-cut in the program shape — the color of the page beneath shows through. The brand is something you literally look through. Click a sheet to set the page's state.

02 — Pattern

The shapes multiply into texture.

For lanyards, totes, hoarding, and digital backgrounds — the three shapes repeat in strict rows, one color per surface. From far away it's texture; up close it's the institution.

Wallpaper · hoarding · lanyards One color per surface
03 — Rules

How the lens behaves.

Black hides, the mark reveals

The default surface is black or white. Color only ever appears through a shape — never as a flat field with nothing cut into it.

One window per piece

A poster gets one cut. A screen gets one lens. Multiplying windows turns a lens into confetti.

Always something behind

The window is never empty decoration — it must reveal content: a word, a face, a venture, a color that means something.
